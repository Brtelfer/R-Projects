In total, these are the items I have opted to remove and the following reasons why:
    - Q377 (RFG):
        - Removed becasue it has a low SD, low total item correlation, near zero intercorrelations with other items, very low factor loading, very high mean and a very low individual correlation with GPA.
        
        While the item: "I feel successful when I am able to answer a difficult question that most others get wrong." on the surface appears to be very similar to other items such as Q387, it has a social comparison aspect, making it very similar to items Q327, Q387 and Q341.
        All of these items pertain to getting explicitly getting 'higher' outcomes than one's peers. The only notable difference I discovered, was the "most others get wrong" part.
        My guess is that most of the items represent doing well in a more broad sense, and others lean towards doing well relative to those around you. 
        That is, some people might be scoring differently because they moreso care about looking good relative to those in their social sphere.

    - Q395 (CS):
        - Removed because of low item-total r, intercorrelations, factor loading and validity
       
        If we look at the item, while it does mention compettitiveness, it may also be assessing something else, like creativity, imagination or an intollerance for boredom.
        If we look at the EFA, we can see a mild loading onto CS and no loading onto RFG which suggests that it might explain another factor partially. 
        Hence, I would either change this item or remove it.

    - Considereded removing Q414 (CS) because of a moderate gender diff. 
    However after looking at the item, there does not appear to be any reason to suspect why it would be unfair to one gender.
    
    - Strongly debated removing Q248 becasue of the low Item-total r and intercorrelations, but it actually predicts GPA. The item is clearly very different.
    That is, it implies a lower aversion to failure and struggle, something essential to learning.and mastery. I personally think that this item might measure.
    Performance goal orientation moreso than mastery orientation, which is why this item predicts GPA, but does not load well with the other items. 
    I think the same could be said about Q198.

        - So, if I wanted to predict GPA, I would keep it. 
        If I wanted to predict compettitiveness, I would remove the item and tweak all items that conflate authority with superiority in RFG (like 377).

    For both RFG and CS, removing these items did not appear to have a substantial impact of the predictive power of these scores on GPA.
